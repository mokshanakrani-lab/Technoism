rules of the arena

Guidelines

Read this before you commit a problem statement — it covers eligibility, conduct, and submission format.

01Eligibility

  • Open to all currently enrolled college / university students
  • Teams of 3–5 members, cross-branch and cross-year teams welcome
  • Each participant may be part of only one team
  • One problem statement commitment per team, made at the Qualifier Round

02Code of Conduct

  • Be respectful to organizers, mentors, and fellow participants
  • All submitted work must be original and built during the event window
  • Use of AI tools is allowed, but must be disclosed in your submission
  • Plagiarism or reused prior projects lead to disqualification
  • Jury and organizing committee decisions are final

03Submission Format

  • Qualifier Round: idea document / deck outlining problem fit and approach
  • Final Showdown: working prototype with a live demo for the jury
  • Include a short README covering setup and tech stack

04On the Day

  • Bring a laptop, charger, and college ID card
  • Arrive during the 08:00–09:00 registration window
  • Starter kit and seat allocation happen at check-in
  • Lunch and refreshments are provided through the round
problem statements

8 problems. Pick your fight.

Choose the statement your team is most excited to solve — teams commit to one at the Qualifier Round.

PS 01

Voice-Activated Accessible Lecture Note Taker

Convert spoken lectures into organized, usable notes in real time — built for students with visual, hearing, or motor impairments.

PS 02

Automated Smart Resume Parser & Mock Interviewer

Read and understand resumes, match candidates to relevant roles, and simulate interview scenarios with feedback.

PS 03

Smart Health Tracker

Monitor relevant health metrics and surface clear, actionable insights with reliable, consistent data handling.

PS 04

AI for Everyone

Design an AI-powered tool that's genuinely useful and accessible to everyday, non-technical people.

PS 05

Digital Addiction

Help users understand, manage, or reduce excessive screen and digital device usage with practical, non-intrusive nudges.

PS 06

Campus Verse

An interactive virtual campus platform for students, parents, faculty, and visitors — seamless navigation, rich location detail, and a secure admin dashboard.

PS 07

Green Tech

Tackle an environmental challenge — sustainability, waste management, energy efficiency, or climate impact — with measurable impact.

PS 08

Women Safety Tech

Improve personal safety and security for women in daily life with a fast, reliable response mechanism.

about the event

Innovation begins with one bold idea.

TECHNOISM is a two-stage hackathon where ambitious minds come together to design, develop, and deliver impactful solutions to real-world challenges. Starting with an online qualifier and culminating in an exciting offline finale, the event celebrates creativity, teamwork, and technical excellence.

From your first line of code to your final pitch, TECHNOISM is where extraordinary ideas become reality.
